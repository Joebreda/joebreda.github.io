About Me

I am a third year PhD student at the University of Washington Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science and Engineer.

I work in the Ubiquitous Computing Lab where I am advised by Shwetak Patel.

I also make music with my guitar and synthesizer as well as digital art using Blender.

I got my B.S. in Computer Engineering with a Minor in Computer Science from the University of Massachusetts Amherst where worked with Jay Taneja, Prashant Shenoy, and David Irwin.

Interests

I'm broadly interested in leveraging big data, ubiquitous technology, and artificial intelligence for social good. I'm passionate about having a positive impact on sustainability, public health, and equitable and efficient urbanism.

Concretely this manifests in my work as:

1) Developing novel sensing and crowdsourcing techniques for both infrastructure and health applications by leveraging smartphones and wearables.

2) Applying machine learning to large social and environmental datasets to understand human behavior and societies impact on the environment.

3) Using ubiquitous data sources such as satelite images or ground based cameras to study both the natural and built environments.

social media, built environment, behavior science.
